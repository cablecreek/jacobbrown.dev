The 2 biggest benefits of using nvim for me are:
 1. **”you don’t know what you don’t know”**: writing your config, tinkering, being curious about nvim will teach you a lot about software.
 2. **it’s truely yours**: you configure it exactly how you want it. Don’t like an aspect of your editor -> remove it, want an ultra specific tool in your editor -> build it. 

What nvim will not do:
- **save time**: you’ll be quicker, but you will spend hours learning and playing around with your setup
- **Do everything for you**: the temptation to have lots of plugins is strong, resist it and keep your config simple.
